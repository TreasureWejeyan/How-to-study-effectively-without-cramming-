Why cramming fails

Cramming tricks your brain into short-term memory. You may recall facts for a few hours, but by exam day, most information fades. Instead, you need spaced, active learning.

A smarter study system

  1. Break it down → Divide topics into small, manageable parts.
  2. Active recall → Test yourself instead of rereading notes.
  3. Spaced repetition → Review at increasing intervals (Day 1, 3, 7…).
  4. Interleaving → Mix subjects (Math → History → Math again).
  5. Pomodoro timing → 25 minutes study, 5 minutes break.
  6. Weekly check-in → Reflect on what stuck, adjust weak areas.

Weekly study plan (no cramming)

Monday–Friday: 2 focused hours daily → new content + recall practice.
Saturday: Mock test and corrections.
Sunday: Quick review of the hardest topics.

Exam week game plan

Focus on reviewing summaries, flashcards, and practice questions. Avoid learning anything completely new. Sleep at least 7–8 hours — memory consolidates while you rest.

Common mistakes to avoid

  • Highlighting without testing yourself.
  • Studying passively (just reading).
  • Skipping breaks or pulling all-nighters.

FAQ

How many hours should I study daily?
Around 2–4 focused hours is enough if you’re consistent.

Can I pass if the exam is in two weeks?
Yes! Focus on high-yield topics + active recall + spaced review.

What if I forget quickly?
That’s normal — repeat using spaced repetition and recall practice.
